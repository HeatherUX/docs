## Is AI generating good content for you?

Using AI in EdTech to generate student materials, tutor and mentor students, create lesson plans, etc is growing. But how do we know that these materials are "good"?

Are they:

- Pedagogically sound
- Age and grade appropriate
- Grounded in learning science
- Contributing to a growth mindset
- And so on?

The only way to know is to measure it. That's where Evaluators comes in.

---

## What are evaluators?

With CZI's Evaluators, you can measure the quality of materials created by AI-powered EdTech apps. Each set of evaluators measures aspects of pedagogical alignment, help identify areas for improvement, and help design consistent user experiences.

Evaluators is part of the [Chan Zuckerberg Initiative's AI Developer Tools for Education](https://chanzuckerberg.com/education/ai/). Our goal is to help developers build more effective, evidence-based tools that support student learning and whole child development. We're working to close the gap between cutting-edge AI and learning science by:

- **Anchoring in learning science**\
  We collaborate with experts at the forefront of learning science and rubric development like Student Achievement Partners, CAST, and ANET.
- **Working with pedagogical experts**\
  We do the hard work of understanding how experts think and co-creating ground-truth datasets that reflect that expertise
- **Building, verifying, and shipping**\
  We are building software that can automatically score any given text the way an expert would.

## Our approach to building an evaluator

We are building evaluators that rigorously measure a variety of dimensions that define what makes "good" AI generated material.

We've built a rigorous, repeatable process for defining and building evaluators that EdTech companies can use to inspect their AI generated output.
